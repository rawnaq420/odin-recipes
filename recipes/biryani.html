Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant South Asian rice dish that combines aromatic basmati rice with meat, fish, or vegetables and a blend of spices. Slow-cooked to perfection, it features layers of flavors from ingredients like saffron, cardamom, and cinnamon. This popular dish comes in many regional varieties and is often served at special occasions, accompanied by sides like raita or salan.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable stock or beef
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin

Directions

  1. Marinate the meat (if using) in yogurt and spices.
  2. Partially cook the basmati rice with whole spices.
  3. In a separate pot, cook the marinated meat or vegetables with onions and more spices.
  4. Layer the partially cooked rice over the meat or vegetable mixture.
  5. Add saffron-infused milk and ghee on top.
  6. Seal the pot tightly and cook on low heat (dum method) until the rice is fully cooked and flavors meld.
  7. Let it rest briefly, then gently mix the layers before serving.
  8. Serve with raita and salad.
